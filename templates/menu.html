We were riding in my father’s pickup truck…

on our way to the nearest library. There were no books where I grew up; you had to travel to find books. I said to my father, “When I grow up I want to live where houses are attached to each other.” I realize now I was longing for something beyond our enormous farm. Isolated, in Appalachia, I had imagined other worlds. Perhaps it was those wide open fields and farmland, feral and free, that allowed my ideas to blossom, along with the books that my father encouraged.

I traveled first to California and eventually made my way to London. And it was there I found those houses “attached to each other,” and eleven-million people to keep me company, just like I had dreamed of.

Peripatetic, an artist and lover of life, I lived abroad for almost two decades. Like a hard- won badge of honor, I cherished my Artist and Writer’s Visa. Europe was good to me. I had followed my heart.

Years later, while working for a client in Northern California, I was asked to take a look at their farm. An 1880’s vernacular Italian homestead, a heritage dry-farm vineyard, an abandoned place, a farm gone fallow. Little did I know I was to follow the tradition of my family and become a fifth generation farmer.

This is the story of a farm’s resurrection.

“When the past meets the future, you know you are in the right place.”
